Three Views of the Bracket

Each view has different needs. Here's what changing each one means.

VIEW 1 — Public spectator /view/[uuid]

Read-only. People watch on their phones or on a projected screen. No interaction required.

Changing this: Straightforward — replace the SVG cards with a proper left-to-right tree. This is the core of Phase 9 and has no complications.

VIEW 2 — Projected display /view/[uuid]/display

Read-only. Same as public view but rendered large for a TV or projector. Also no interaction.

Changing this: Same as the public view — just apply the new bracket component with larger text/spacing. Also straightforward.

VIEW 3 — Organizer score entry /manage/[uuid]

This is the unknown unknown. The organizer taps a match to enter the score. Right now, matches are listed as tappable cards — easy to target on a phone.

The challenge:

In a traditional bracket tree, each match box is small — just big enough to show two names. Tapping one to enter a score would work, but the tap targets get very small on a phone, especially in early rounds with many matches. The current card layout was deliberately made large for courtside use.

Two ways to handle this:

  1. Tap-to-open: Show the bracket tree visually, but tapping a match opens a larger score-entry panel (modal or drawer). You see the bracket, tap the match you just finished, and a big UI pops up to record it.
  2. Keep the current layout: Organizer view stays as large tappable cards. Only public + projected views get the new tree. Simpler, but the organizer doesn't see the bracket visually.
